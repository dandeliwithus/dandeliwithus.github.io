Sightseeing in Dandeli

Syntheri Rocks:

monolithic single granite boulder with a height of 300ft with the river Kaneri flowing at its core. It is believed to have formed due to volcanic eruptions several years ago. It is named after an English lady called Ms Cinthera, who is believed to have discovered this place a century ago. Ideal for tourists to visit and geologists to research on this giant hidden structure amidst the woods.

Eco Park:

The Moulangi Eco Park is a popular tourist spot in Dandeli. Situated amidst the dense forests, surrounded by a pristine natural aura with huge bamboo trees, it is ideal for adventure lovers and campers. About 11 km away from the Wilderness camp in Kali, this Eco Park is more than a picnic spot. The forests are home to many varieties of species of flora and fauna. It is a bliss for the wildlife and plant lovers. Trekkers can enjoy the steep cliffs. Rock climbers can indulge in climbing the large rock formations. Friends and family could go there for a calm picnic in the lap of nature.

Crocodile park:

This is a private property somewhat appropriately fenced where you can see crocks in their natural habitat. The owner of this place is the guide and he will help you to spot crocks at a nominal entry fee of INR 20/person. In this vlog form 4:52 mins crocks can be seen.

cartoon park:

Renowned for its famous statues of Hollywood characters and Indian cartoons, Dandakaranya Park is one of the best tourist spots in Dandeli that makes for an interesting visit. With an intriguing story leading up to its establishment, Dandakaranya Eco Park is often cited as a great place to enjoy some time with your loved ones. It was built to stop land grabbing and illegal encroachment of the forest areas and today serves as a modern example of sustainable tourism in Dandeli. It is now a theme park that attracts a number of children and boasts of having their favourite characters come alive be it the Avengers, Transformers, The Simpsons, Charlie Chaplin and Indian characters like Chhota Bheem and Motu Patlu.

supa dam backwater:

This is the highest dam of the Kali river valley project located at Ganesh Gudi. Vast expanse of the back waters can be viewed from vantage points. The river downstream is a place for adventure sports. If you are really lucky, you may be able to sight some wildlife. Fabulous place for nature lovers. Must watch, especially in the peak of the monsoon. Great views towards the end of the monsoon when the reservoir is almost full.
